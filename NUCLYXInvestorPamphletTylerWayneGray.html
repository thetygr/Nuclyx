The Technology
Two Products. One Chemistry.

Nuclyx technology is built on engineered graphene oxide — a single-atom-thick carbon lattice whose carboxyl, hydroxyl, and epoxide surface groups provide pharmaceutical-grade molecular recognition of radioactive elements at parts-per-trillion concentrations.

🧪
Tritovex
Spray Encapsulation Technology
A sprayable graphene oxide aqueous suspension delivered by radiation-hardened robot directly onto contaminated corium surfaces. Self-assembles into a crosslinked barrier film — 7 nm thick, five orders of magnitude less permeable than conventional materials. Simultaneously chelates radionuclides chemically and physically seals them from aqueous leaching. Self-healing particle reservoirs autonomously repair radiation-induced cracks.

Membyx
Isotope Separation Membrane
A graphene-Nafion-PTFE composite membrane exploiting the quantum mechanical isotope effect — tritium moves 3.1× slower than hydrogen through a graphene lattice at 0.064 nm pore size. Tyler's innovation: engineered PTFE reinforcement geometry suppresses Nafion swelling-induced graphene delamination, the failure mode that prevented all prior architectures from operating continuously at industrial flow rates.
